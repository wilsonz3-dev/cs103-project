Background

Town Overview: Bayridge

Once a mid-sized shipping town, now known as a testing center for new artificial intelligence products. The town's economy revolves around maintaining automated supply lines, managing data centers, and supporting tech contracted by federal and corporate agencies.

Population

Bayridge’s population sits just under 100,000. It skews older, with many residents choosing to stay despite better opportunities in metropolitan cores. The younger crowd tend to work in tech-adjacent maintenance roles, or provide support services for the few industries that haven’t gone completely virtual.

Characteristics

Bayridge is modest, functional, and just a bit worn at the edges. Public transit is AI-run and sparsely used. Most buildings are retrofitted with climate-stabilizing panels, and even the old brick libraries now host charging stations and telepresence booths.

Economics and Resources

The main resources comes from logistics maintenance contracts, data storage facilities, and AI training centers. Bob works for a contracted maintenance company that ensures autonomous delivery drones remain compliant with federal safety regulations.

Landscape

The town is situated along a coastal area once dominated by large-scale wind farms, many of which are now outdated and barely operational. The few remaining green spaces are genetically engineered to endure both fluctuating temperatures and extreme weather, ensuring that the town can survive even in a shifting climate.

Key Events in the Story

Event 1: Drone Malfunction

A delivery drone malfunctions mid-route and crashes near the town’s residential sector. Bob’s called in to cover it up before local news picks it up.

Event 2: Layoffs

Bob’s department is restructured after a new AI system reclassifies several departments as redundant, causing his workload to double.
